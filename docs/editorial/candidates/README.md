# Candidate Memos

Candidate memos are working editorial records created by the editor-in-chief when monthly volume planning starts. They are developed across three Monday editorial meetings and are not approved plans.

For Vol. 002 and later, use one planning branch and one draft pull request from research through finalization. The candidate memo stays on that branch until the fourth-Monday final meeting.

## Monthly Stages

- Second Monday, `planning:research`: perform current web research and record demand signals. Do not create the approved plan.
- Third Monday, `planning:shortlist`: refresh the research, compare themes, and select a provisional direction. Do not create the approved plan.
- Fourth Monday, `planning:finalize`: refresh the research again, lock the theme and lineup, then create the approved plan.

## Required Structure

Candidate memos for Vol. 002 and later must include:

- `調査更新日: YYYY-MM-DD`
- `## 調査ログ`
- `## ウェブ調査による需要シグナル`
- `## 読者需要の仮説`
- `## テーマ候補`
- `## 季節感`
- `## 記事構成候補`
- `## 参照情報`

Each meeting must run current web searches rather than relying only on editorial intuition. Record at least three `検索語:` entries and at least four dated source URLs across at least three source types, such as search-demand data, official weather or calendar information, fashion or lifestyle industry reporting, editorial coverage, or public consumer discussion.

Use this source format:

`- 種別: 検索需要 | 確認日: 2026-07-13 | URL: https://example.com/ | 要点: ...`

Web trends are evidence of current demand, but they are not automatic editorial instructions. Keep evidence-backed demand signals separate from editorial hypotheses, and explain how KOTATSU turns short-lived interest into a calm, useful, longer-lived article. When current web research is unavailable, record the limitation and do not finalize the plan from invented trends.

Naming:

- `vol-001.md`
- `vol-002.md`

For Vol. 001, the memo remains a legacy inaugural record and is exempt from the staged research schema.
